About Strength Faith Builds

Strength Faith builds in Elden Ring blend high Strength for heavy weapon damage with Faith for incantations like healing and buffs. These hybrids offer tanky playstyles with magical utility, perfect for bosses and exploration.

Key advantages include self-sustainability through healing spells and strong physical defense. However, they require careful stat investment to balance both attributes effectively.

Why Choose Strength Faith?

  • Versatility: Switch between melee combat and magic support seamlessly.
  • Self-Healing: Use incantations like Heal or Greater Heal to sustain yourself without wasting healing flasks.
  • Buffs: Apply Golden Order's Seal and other blessings to increase damage and defense.
  • Tankiness: High Vigor and heavy armor make these builds durable against most threats.

Example Build: Paladin Hybrid

Build Overview

The Paladin is the quintessential Strength Faith build, balancing offensive power with healing support. This is perfect for solo play and cooperative multiplayer.

Stat Distribution (Level 150)

  • Vigor: 50 — Essential for surviving boss attacks.
  • Mind: 20 — Enough FP for 2-3 healing casts between rests.
  • Endurance: 25 — Allows heavy armor and weapon usage without fat-rolling.
  • Strength: 55 — Maximize heavy weapon scaling (Claymore, Greatsword).
  • Dexterity: 12 — Meet basic weapon requirements.
  • Intelligence: 10 — Base stat only.
  • Faith: 35 — Unlock Heal, Greater Heal, Golden Order's Seal, and basic buffs.
  • Arcane: 10 — Base stat only.

Weapons & Gear

Right Hand: Claymore (heavy affinity) or Greatsword. Both scale excellently with high Strength.

Left Hand: Erdtree Seal or Giant-Crusher Seal for incantation boost.

Armor: Tree Sentinel set (medium weight) or Bull-Goat's Talisman for poise and defense.

Talismans:

  • Radagon's Seal (boosts incantation scaling)
  • Ritual Pot Talisman (reduces FP consumption)
  • Millicent's Prosthesis (increases dexterity scaling)
  • Scorpion Charm (boosts critical damage)

Incantations & Spells

  • Heal: 155 FP recovery, essential for solo play.
  • Greater Heal: 259 FP recovery, use when you have breathing room.
  • Golden Order's Seal: Buff that increases physical and magic damage for 60 seconds.
  • Blessing of the Erdtree: Grants 10% stat boost for all attributes.
  • Flame, Grant Me Strength: Massive attack boost; powerful for boss fights.

Combat Strategy

Boss Fights: Stay aggressive with 2-3 hit combos, then back off to heal if below 50% health. Use buffs before major attacks.

PvE Exploration: Keep heals queued; use weapon arts for crowd control.

Multiplayer: Act as a support tank—maintain aggro and heal cooperators when needed.

Popular Strength Faith Builds

1. Paladin (Balanced Hybrid)

Focus: 50 STR, 35 FTH | Role: Tank with healing support

Best for players who want a mix of melee damage, defense, and utility. Excels in solo play with self-healing.

2. Berserker Priest (Offensive Hybrid)

Focus: 60+ STR, 25-30 FTH | Role: High-damage dealer with buffs

Sacrifices some survivability for massive weapon damage. Use buffs like Flame, Grant Me Strength before major engagements. Requires more skill to manage healing between attacks.

3. Holy Knight (Defensive Hybrid)

Focus: 45 STR, 40+ FTH | Role: Tanky incantation user

Prioritizes Faith for more incantation variety. Gains access to more healing and defensive spells. Trade some physical damage for magical utility and survivability.

4. Vagabond (Pure Tank)

Focus: 55+ STR, 20-25 FTH | Role: Unkillable tank

Max out physical defense with heavy armor and poise. Faith is just enough for emergency heals. Best for PvP or challenging bosses where durability matters most.

Stat Distribution Guide

Level 100 (Beginner)

  • Vigor: 40 | Endurance: 20 | Strength: 45 | Faith: 20
  • Remaining points: Dexterity (12), Mind (15), others base.

Level 150 (Intermediate)

  • Vigor: 50 | Endurance: 25 | Strength: 55 | Faith: 35
  • Fine-tune other stats based on your playstyle.

Level 200+ (Advanced)

  • Vigor: 60 | Endurance: 30 | Strength: 70 | Faith: 45
  • Add Mind (25) for more incantation casts.

Best Weapons for Strength Faith

Two-Handed Strength Weapons (Recommended)

  • Claymore: Balanced, good scaling, versatile moveset.
  • Greatsword: Heavy hitter; slow but devastating damage.
  • Maliketh's Black Blade: Scales with both STR and FTH; unique moveset.
  • Radahn's Greatsword: High damage but heavy requirements.

Seals (Left Hand)

  • Erdtree Seal: Boosts all incantations equally.
  • Giant-Crusher Seal: Best for pure Faith scaling.
  • Finger Seal: Lightweight, good for spellcasters.

Getting Started with Strength Faith

Early Game (Level 1-50)

  1. Start as Vagabond or Samurai for better initial stats.
  2. Level Vigor to 20 and Strength to 20 immediately.
  3. Find a basic seal to cast heals (purchased from vendors in Roundtable Hold).
  4. Farm Runes at Mohgwyn Palace or Caelid for faster leveling.
  5. Don't worry about Faith until you have a stronger weapon.

Mid Game (Level 50-120)

  1. Upgrade Strength to 40+ and Vigor to 35+.
  2. Switch weapons to Claymore or Greatsword once found (around Liurnia).
  3. Allocate 20-25 points to Faith to unlock key incantations.
  4. Craft and use Flask of Crimson Tears as backup healing.
  5. Defeat major bosses to unlock higher-level incantations.

End Game (Level 120+)

  1. Optimize your stat split based on your playstyle.
  2. Collect and equip optimal talismans (Radagon's Seal, Ritual Pot).
  3. Master positioning: stay close but punish openings.
  4. Learn boss attack patterns to time heals properly.

Incantations Ranking by Importance

Must-Have (Start Here)

  • Heal: Basic self-healing spell. Unlock immediately.
  • Golden Order's Seal: All-purpose buff that increases damage and defense.

Highly Recommended

  • Greater Heal: More powerful healing; takes 3-4 seconds to cast.
  • Blessing of the Erdtree: 10% stat boost; lasts 60 seconds.
  • Flame, Grant Me Strength: Massive attack boost; pair with weapon art for huge damage.

Advanced (Optional)

  • Bloodhound's Step: Quick dodge tool; costs 1 FP per cast.
  • Holy Ground: Area healing for multiplayer; heals allies near you.
  • Dragonclaw: Offensive incantation for crowd control.

Pros and Cons of Strength Faith Builds

Pros:

  • Versatile Combat: Switch between aggressive melee and supportive magic on the fly.
  • Self-Sufficient: Healing spells eliminate reliance on healing flasks, allowing more aggressive play.
  • Tanky: High Vigor and heavy armor make you extremely durable against physical and magical attacks.
  • Effective in PvE and PvP: Heals make solo bosses manageable; buffs help in multiplayer.
  • Flexible Scaling: Heavy weapons scale excellently with Strength; incantations scale with Faith.

Cons:

  • Stat Split: Cannot maximize damage in one area; requires careful investment.
  • FP Management: Incantations consume Focus Points quickly; you need to rest between major engagements.
  • Weight Management: Heavy weapons and armor drain Endurance; requires balancing load.
  • Casting Time: Heals and buffs take 2-4 seconds; use during boss downtime only.
  • Less Burst Damage: Not as devastating as pure Strength builds in a single hit.

Pro Tips for Success

Know Your Limits: Heal when below 60% health to maintain offensive pressure.

Buff Before Combat: Cast Golden Order's Seal before entering boss fights.

Manage FP: Don't waste FP on heals if you can dodge; save it for emergencies.

Practice Positioning: Stay close but ready to retreat for safety.
